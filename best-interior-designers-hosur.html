Hiring Right

How to Evaluate Interior Designers in Hosur

Before you sign any agreement or transfer any money, go through this checklist. It will take you an hour but save you from months of regret.

1

Ask for photos of completed homes, not renders

Any designer can show you beautiful 3D renders made by software. Ask for actual photos of homes they have handed over. Even better, ask if you can visit one.

2

Call 2-3 past clients

Ask the firm for references you can contact directly. If they cannot provide any, that tells you something. Ask those clients: was the project on time? Did the cost match the quote? Would you hire them again?

3

Demand a written BOQ with exact brand names

The Bill of Quantities should name the brand of plywood, the grade of hardware, the finish on the paint. "Best quality" is not a specification. "Greenply 18mm BWP" is.

4

Ask if they offer 3D visualization

Top interior designers in Hosur will show you room-by-room 3D views before any work begins. This is your chance to change your mind before money is spent on materials.

5

Understand what happens if costs overrun

A fixed-price contract means the designer absorbs cost overruns from their side. A rough estimate means you pay more if anything changes. Know which one you are signing.

Questions People Ask Us

How do I choose the best interior designer in Hosur? +

Ask to see photos of completed homes, not just renders. Ask for two or three references from past clients you can call. Check that they give you a written BOQ listing exact brands and quantities before taking any payment. A reliable interior designer in Hosur will give you a 3D room preview and a fixed-price contract with milestone-based payments.

What questions should I ask before hiring an interior designer? +

Ask: Do you do 3D visualization before work starts? Can you share a written BOQ with brand names? Who supervises the work on-site? What is your payment schedule? Do you have a warranty on the woodwork? What happens if the cost goes over budget? These questions will quickly tell you how professional the firm is.

What is the difference between an interior designer and an interior decorator? +

An interior designer plans the layout, storage, material specifications, lighting, and coordinates with civil and electrical work. An interior decorator mainly handles colours, furniture, and accessories. Design Intend is a registered architecture firm, so our interior work is planned at a structural and technical level, not just surface decoration.
