Common Problems

What People Experience With Bad Designers in Hosur

This is not to scare you. It is to help you recognise warning signs before you sign a contract. These are the situations we hear about from clients who came to us after a bad first experience.

Verbal Quotes That Grow After Work Starts

A designer quotes Rs 9 lakh verbally for a 3BHK. Work starts. Then additional charges appear for false ceiling framing, electrical points, wall putty, and tile wastage. The final bill is Rs 13.5 lakh. None of this was in writing. With no signed BOQ, there is no way to dispute the additions.

No Written Design Before Execution

Some designers in Hosur skip the 3D design step and jump straight to fabrication. When the wardrobe arrives and the colour or dimension is not what you imagined, it is too late. Fixing fabricated items costs money. Seeing a 3D design costs nothing.

Materials Substituted Mid-Project

The quote said Hettich channels for the kitchen drawers. The carpenter shows up with a no-name brand because "the shop ran out." You do not notice until the drawers start wobbling 8 months later. Without a BOQ that names the brand, you have no recourse.

Poor Supervision

The designer visits the site once to start the job, and again to collect final payment. In between, untrained labour makes decisions about tile alignment, cabinet heights, and switch positions. The result looks passable in photos but fails in daily use.

Your Checklist

How to Evaluate Interior Designers Before Hiring

Use this checklist before you sign with any interior designer in Hosur.

01

Look at Completed Homes, Not Just Renders

Ask to see photos of real completed projects, not just 3D visualisations or showroom setups. Ask the designer how many homes of your type they have completed in the last 2 years in Hosur or nearby areas.

02

Call 2 to 3 Past Clients

Ask the designer for references and actually call them. Ask: Did the final cost match the quote? Was work done on time? Did the designer solve problems when they came up? Would you hire them again? Genuine references are worth more than any portfolio.

03

Demand a Written BOQ with Brand Names

The BOQ must specify: plywood grade (19mm BWR minimum), hardware brand (Hettich, Hafele, Ebco), countertop material, tile brand and price range, and paint brand. If a designer refuses to name brands, they are leaving room to substitute later.

04

Get a 3D Design First

Any reputable interior designer will provide 3D views of each room before fabrication starts. If a designer says they do not do 3D or charge separately for it as an extra, that is a red flag. The 3D design protects you from unhappy surprises.

05

Ask What Happens If You Go Over Budget

A fixed-price contract means the designer absorbs the risk of material price changes. A rate-based contract passes that risk to you. Know which one you are signing.

What is the difference between an interior designer and an interior decorator in Hosur? +

An interior designer handles space planning, technical drawings, and manages execution including carpentry, electrical, and tiling. An interior decorator focuses on styling: colours, furniture arrangement, accessories. For a full home project in Hosur including kitchen cabinets, wardrobes, false ceiling, and flooring, you need a designer or design-build firm, not just a decorator.
