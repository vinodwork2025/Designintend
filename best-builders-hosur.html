Common Problems

Problems With Bad Builders in Hosur

These are real situations. Understanding them helps you avoid them.

Vanishing After 50 Percent Payment

The contractor seems reliable, starts work well, collects 50 to 60 percent of the total amount, and then slows down dramatically. By the time the client realises the project is being abandoned, the contractor is on to the next job. Legal recourse is slow and expensive. The best protection is a milestone payment structure that never lets you pay ahead of completed work.

Mixing Substandard Materials

Your contract says TATA or JSW steel. You visit the site and see bars with unfamiliar markings, or no markings at all. Without purchase receipts and daily site supervision, you cannot know what went into your foundation. Substandard steel and concrete do not show their defects for 5 to 10 years. By then, legal recourse against a contractor is nearly impossible.

No ISI Certification on Key Materials

ISI certification on steel bars, electrical wire, and cement is required by Indian standards. Some contractors buy uncertified material at a lower price and pass on a fraction of the saving as a lower quote, keeping the rest as margin. Always ask for purchase receipts and ISI certification during construction, not just in the quote document.

Poor Concrete Quality from No Supervision

Concrete strength depends on the water-cement ratio, mixing time, and curing. An unsupervised site often has workers who add too much water to make mixing easier, reducing strength by 20 to 30 percent. No structural test before the pour, no cube tests after. By the time the slab is cast, there is no way to redo it.
