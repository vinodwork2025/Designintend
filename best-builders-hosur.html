What to Watch Out For

Common Problems With Builders in Hosur

Most horror stories about construction in Hosur follow a predictable pattern. Recognising these warning signs early keeps you safe.

Disappearing after 50% payment

An individual contractor asks for 60-70% of the total cost upfront. Once that money is in hand, progress slows. Calls go unanswered. Work stops. Recovering your money or finishing the project becomes a legal nightmare.

Material substitution without telling you

You discussed using 43-grade cement. The builder quietly uses 33-grade because it costs less. You quoted Ultratech — he uses an unbranded local mix. The difference shows up years later in cracks and seepage, by which time the builder is long gone.

No supervision on-site

The builder assigns an untrained mason or labourer to manage the site. No qualified engineer checks the concrete mix ratio, the rebar placement, or the curing schedule. Structural quality suffers, and it is invisible until something fails.

No written contract

A verbal agreement means the scope changes every week. "Oh, this was extra" becomes a common phrase. Without a written fixed-price contract, you have no protection when the builder says the final bill is 30% higher than originally discussed.

Payment Schedule

How We Structure Payments

Our milestone-based payment structure is designed to protect you. You never pay significantly ahead of the work completed. Here is exactly how it works:

1

10% — On signing + materials advance

This covers the initial materials procurement and mobilisation. Work begins within a few days of this payment.

2

20% — On foundation completion

Paid after you inspect and approve the completed foundation. The most critical structural element is verified before you proceed.

3

20% — On ground floor slab

After the ground floor roof slab is cast and cured. You can see the actual structure taking shape.

4

20% — On first floor slab (if applicable)

For G+1 or G+2 buildings only. Paid after the next slab is complete.

5

15% — On roof and terrace completion

Structural work is fully complete at this stage. You pay for completed structure, not promises.

6

10% — On finishing work

Covers electrical, plumbing, flooring, and painting. Paid as finishing progresses.

7

5% — On final handover

The last 5% is retained until you do a final walk-through and approve the completed home. This keeps us focused on finishing strong.

Questions About Hiring Builders in Hosur

How do I find a reliable builder in Hosur? +

Look for a registered firm with a permanent office in or near Hosur, not an individual contractor with just a phone number. Ask to see 2-3 completed projects you can visit. Ask for references from past clients and actually call them. Ask for a written fixed-price contract before paying anything beyond an initial token.

What are the red flags when hiring a builder in Hosur? +

Red flags include: asking for more than 30-40% of the total cost upfront; no written contract or BOQ; no permanent office address; unable to provide references; using vague terms like "best quality" instead of naming specific materials; no site supervisor during construction; and no warranty offered post-handover.

What is a reasonable payment schedule for home construction? +

A fair milestone-based payment schedule looks like this: 10% on signing and materials advance; 20% on foundation completion; 20% on ground floor slab; 20% on first floor slab if applicable; 15% on roof; 10% on finishing work including electrical and plumbing; and 5% on final handover. You should never pay more than the work value already completed.
