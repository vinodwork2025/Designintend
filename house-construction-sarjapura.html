Who Builds in Sarjapura

Custom Home Construction in Sarjapura

Sarjapura sits in the southeast Bengaluru growth corridor, between Electronic City and Whitefield. The area has seen rapid residential development over the last decade. But unlike Bagalur or Hosur where factory workers build modest homes, Sarjapura's typical home builder is an IT professional in their late 30s or early 40s who has bought a plot and wants a well-designed custom home.

These clients have specific expectations. They want an architect who understands design, not just drawings. They want transparent pricing in a proper contract, not a verbal quote per sq ft. They want a home office, an open kitchen that connects to the living room, good natural light, and materials that do not look cheap. They are not price-insensitive. They are quality-sensitive.

Design Intend was started by architects with this kind of client in mind. We have worked in Sarjapura and the surrounding Bengaluru fringe areas of Attibele, Anekal, and Chandapura. We understand the Karnataka approval system and know how to deliver a home that meets a professional's expectations without inflated city-firm pricing.

The problems Sarjapura plot owners face

  • Bengaluru design firms charge Rs 80 to Rs 120 per sq ft for architecture fees alone before any construction starts. The design itself costs Rs 2 to Rs 3 lakh for a standard home.
  • Many contractors in the Sarjapura area are small local firms with no structural engineering or quality testing process. Slab cube tests? No. Structural engineer approval on reinforcement? No.
  • Karnataka approvals (gram panchayat or BMRDA) take longer than Tamil Nadu DTCP. Builders who quote 12 months often mean 18 months.
  • Black cotton soil patches in some Sarjapura areas need raft foundation or special treatment. Contractors who do not recognise this design a standard footing and the floor slab cracks in year two.
  • The WFH shift has changed what people want in a home office. Many contractors do not understand the electrical, acoustic, and ventilation requirements for a proper work-from-home setup.

Sarjapura Construction Context

What You Need to Know Before Building in Sarjapura

Karnataka approvals: gram panchayat and BMRDA

Sarjapura falls in Anekal taluk in Bengaluru Rural District. The approval authority depends on your exact plot location. Plots in gram panchayat areas need GP building permission. Plots in BMRDA-approved layouts need BMRDA clearance. Some parts of Sarjapura near the CMC (town municipality) boundary need CMC permission.

We verify the correct authority for your plot before preparing any drawings. This step saves months of delay and prevents the common problem of filing with the wrong body. Karnataka approvals generally take 10 to 14 weeks compared to 6 to 10 weeks for Tamil Nadu DTCP.

Soil conditions in Sarjapura

Sarjapura has mixed soil conditions across the area. The northern areas closer to the Bengaluru urban edge tend to have red laterite soil, stable and good for standard foundations. Parts of the southern and eastern Sarjapura belt have black cotton soil, which swells when wet and contracts when dry. This causes floor slab cracking, plinth wall issues, and door frame distortion if the foundation is not designed for it.

We identify the soil type during the site visit. Where black cotton soil is present, we specify a PCC layer with anti-shrink treatment and appropriate footing design. This is a non-negotiable step for any house in this area.

What a Sarjapura premium home looks like

Sarjapura's individual plot homes tend to be larger than Hosur or Bagalur homes. 2,000 to 3,000 sq ft two-floor homes are common. Key features IT professionals typically want in this area:

  • Home office or study with dedicated wiring, acoustic consideration, and natural light
  • Open kitchen connected to dining and living area on the ground floor
  • Large windows and cross-ventilation designed into the floor plan, not added as an afterthought
  • Covered car parking for two vehicles as a structural element, not just a driveway
  • Utility and service areas separated from the main living zones
  • Provision for solar panels on the roof

We design all of this into the floor plan from the start. Not as additions that the contractor builds after the structure is done.

Our Approach

How We Manage a Sarjapura Construction Project

1

Discovery and Plot Assessment

Site visit, soil identification, jurisdiction verification, and a discussion about your requirements. We ask about lifestyle, work-from-home setup, guests, parking, and future plans. The brief informs the design, not the other way around.

2

Architecture and 3D Design

Full floor plan for all floors, elevation drawings, and 3D visualisation of the exterior and key interiors. You approve the design before structural drawings begin. Changes at this stage cost nothing extra.

3

Structural Design and BOQ

A licensed structural engineer designs columns, beams, slabs, and foundation based on your soil type. The Bill of Quantities lists every material, brand, and quantity. These two documents form the basis of the fixed-price contract.

4

Karnataka Approval

We prepare and submit the building plan application with the correct local body. Karnataka approvals take 10 to 14 weeks. We track the application, respond to any queries from the authority, and get the approved plan in hand before construction starts.

5

Construction and Quality Control

Dedicated site supervisor with weekly visits to Sarjapura. Concrete cube testing on every slab pour. Structural engineer sign-off before reinforcement is covered. Weekly photo updates via WhatsApp. Milestone-based payments so you pay only after each stage is visibly complete.

6

Finishing and Interior Handoff

All finishes, electrical, plumbing, painting, and external work. Interior design can continue from the same team after handover. See our interior design service for Sarjapura homes.
