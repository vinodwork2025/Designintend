Common Questions

Everything You Need
to Know Before Building

What is included in the ₹1,950–₹2,300/sq.ft rate? +

The per sq.ft rate covers all major construction works:

  • Structural work — foundation, columns, beams, slabs (RCC M20)
  • Brick masonry, plastering, and waterproofing
  • Flooring (vitrified/marble depending on tier)
  • Electrical wiring, switchboards, and distribution boards
  • Plumbing — water supply and drainage
  • Doors, windows, and grilles
  • Painting — interior and exterior
  • Architect's design and site supervision fees
What is NOT included in the construction cost? +

The following are typically quoted separately:

  • Land purchase cost and registration charges
  • Government plan approval fees (CMDA / Panchayat)
  • Water and electrical connection charges (TANGEDCO / TWAD)
  • Compound wall, main gate, and driveway
  • Borewell or sump construction
  • Interior furniture, curtains, and furnishings
  • Landscaping (Standard tier only — included in Luxury)
  • Generator / solar panels (unless specified)
What factors increase the construction cost? +

Several things can push the cost above the base rate:

  • Soft soil or rocky terrain — requires deeper/costlier foundations
  • Irregular plot shape — more design complexity, more material waste
  • Basement — adds ₹1,200–₹1,500/sq.ft for the basement area
  • Design complexity — curves, cantilevers, large glass facades
  • Material upgrades — imported tiles, premium sanitary brands
  • Mid-project changes — design changes after work begins are costly
  • Material price fluctuations — steel and cement prices vary quarterly
How long does construction take in Hosur? +

Typical timelines for residential construction in Hosur:

  • Design + approval stage: 2–3 months
  • Under 1,200 sq.ft home: 10–12 months total
  • 1,200–2,000 sq.ft home: 12–15 months total
  • Above 2,000 sq.ft: 15–20 months total

Delays are usually caused by late approval from local bodies, monsoon season, or mid-project design changes. Design Intend provides a fixed project schedule before construction begins.

Do you offer turnkey construction in Hosur? +

Yes. Design Intend provides full turnkey construction in Hosur — from initial concept to key handover. This includes:

  • Architectural design and structural drawings
  • Government plan approval (CMDA / local panchayat)
  • Full site construction and supervision
  • Interior design and finishes
  • Electrical, plumbing, and utility commissioning
  • Final punch list and defect rectification

You get a single point of accountability. No managing multiple contractors.

Is construction in Hosur cheaper than Bengaluru? +

Yes — significantly. Construction costs in Hosur are typically 15–25% lower than Bengaluru for equivalent quality. This is because:

  • Lower labour rates in Hosur vs Bengaluru
  • Closer proximity to material suppliers (brick, sand, aggregate)
  • Lower site overhead and logistics costs
  • Less regulatory complexity than BBMP-governed areas

Bengaluru equivalent: Standard ₹2,200–₹2,500/sq.ft · Premium ₹2,600–₹3,000/sq.ft · Luxury ₹3,200+/sq.ft

Is the rate per sq.ft on built-up area or carpet area? +

Our rates are quoted on built-up area (BUA) — the total constructed area including walls, staircase, utility spaces, and balconies.

Carpet area (the usable floor space inside walls) is typically 70–75% of built-up area. So a 2,000 sq.ft built-up home has approximately 1,400–1,500 sq.ft of usable space.

Always ask your architect to clarify which measurement they are quoting on — it makes a significant difference to the final number.

Can I pay in installments during construction? +

Yes. Design Intend follows a milestone-based payment schedule tied to construction progress:

  • 10% — On signing the construction agreement
  • 20% — On completion of foundation and plinth
  • 20% — On completion of structure (columns, beams, slabs)
  • 20% — On completion of masonry and plastering
  • 20% — On completion of flooring, electrical, and plumbing
  • 10% — On final handover and punch-list completion

You only pay when you can see the work done. No advance payments beyond 10%.
