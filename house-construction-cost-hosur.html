What You Get

What Is Included in the Rate

When you see a per sq ft rate, you need to know exactly what is inside it. Here is what the Design Intend rate covers.

Structure and Masonry

RCC frame with M20 or M25 concrete depending on grade. TATA or JSW TMT steel reinforcement. Brick masonry walls with cement mortar. Internal and external plastering, including smooth finish for painting.

Flooring

Vitrified tiles in living, dining, and bedrooms at Standard grade. Anti-skid ceramic tiles in bathrooms and kitchen. Premium and Luxury grades include larger format tiles and better brands such as Kajaria or Johnson.

Electrical and Plumbing

Concealed copper wiring using Finolex or Havells cables. Modular switches and main panel with MCBs. Concealed CPVC plumbing using Astral pipes. Bathroom sanitary fittings from Hindware or Parryware at Standard grade, Jaquar at Premium and Luxury.

Doors and Windows

UPVC windows with mosquito mesh and safety grills. Flush doors with solid wood frames for main and room doors. Bathroom doors with WPC frames. All door hardware included.

Paint and Finish

Two-coat exterior weather shield paint from Asian Paints or Berger. Interior walls with primer and two coats of emulsion. Smooth putty finish on internal walls before painting.

Architect and Supervision

Structural design by a registered architect. Drawing set for DTCP submission. Site supervision during construction to check material quality and workmanship. This is included in the rate, not charged separately.

Not Included

This is why the total cost of building a home is always higher than just the construction rate multiplied by the area. You need to account for all these items upfront when you make your budget.

Cost Factors

Why Construction Costs Vary

Two plots in Hosur, same size, can have very different construction costs. Here are the main reasons.

Soil Type

Hosur has a mix of rocky terrain and clay soil. Rocky areas need more excavation using machines, which adds Rs 80,000 to Rs 1.5 lakh for a typical house. Clay soil may need deeper footings or a raft foundation, which also adds cost. A soil test done before you sign any contract will tell you exactly what your plot needs.

Number of Floors

A single-floor ground level house is the most straightforward to build. Adding a first floor requires a stronger column and beam design, which adds Rs 50 to Rs 100 per sq ft to the overall cost compared to a flat single floor. However, building up instead of wide is often cheaper than buying a larger plot.

Plot Access

If your plot is on a narrow lane or a corner that is hard to reach with a concrete mixer or material truck, expect to pay Rs 30,000 to Rs 80,000 more for manual material handling. Wide road access keeps this cost down.

Material Brand Choices

Choosing Jaquar taps over Parryware, or Italian marble over vitrified tiles, can add Rs 200 to Rs 500 per sq ft easily. The structure is the same. The finish changes the cost significantly.

Foundation Type

Standard isolated footings work for most plots. But if the soil bearing capacity is low, you may need a raft foundation, pile foundation, or deeper footings. This is discovered only after a soil test, which is why skipping the test is a costly mistake.

Smart Budgeting

How to Avoid Hidden Costs

Most budget overruns happen not because of unexpected events, but because the original quote was incomplete. Here is how to protect yourself.

01

Get a Fixed-Price Contract

Insist on a written fixed-price contract that specifies total cost, payment schedule tied to construction milestones, and a clear list of what is included and excluded. A rate-based contract with no ceiling leaves you exposed to cost overruns at every stage.

02

Ask for a Detailed BOQ

A Bill of Quantities lists every item of work: concrete grade, steel brand, tile size, brand, plumbing pipe brand, paint brand. If a contractor gives you only a per sq ft rate with no BOQ, you have no way to hold them to quality. Demand specifics before signing.

03

Pay in Milestones

A fair payment structure ties each payment to a completed stage. Foundation, slab, walls, roofing, finishing. Never pay more than 30 to 40 percent before work starts. If a contractor asks for 60 to 70 percent upfront, walk away.

04

Budget All Additional Costs Upfront

List out every additional cost before you start: DTCP approval, soil test, compound wall, borewell, interior work. A family that budgets Rs 40 lakh for construction is often surprised when the total comes to Rs 55 lakh. It should not be a surprise if you plan correctly.

Hosur Market

Hosur 2025 Construction Market

Hosur sits about 45 kilometres from Bangalore in Krishnagiri District. The city has been growing fast because of industrial investment from companies like Samsung, Foxconn, and Tata Electronics. This has brought more demand for housing, but also more builders into the market, not all of them reliable.

Why Hosur Is Cheaper Than Bangalore

Construction in Hosur is typically 10 to 15 percent cheaper than equivalent construction in Bangalore. Labour costs are lower. Tamil Nadu has several cement plants nearby that keep cement prices competitive. Logistics for material delivery is simpler compared to navigating Bangalore's traffic.

Steel Prices in 2025

After significant fluctuations in 2022 and 2023, TMT steel prices have stabilised in 2025. This makes fixed-price contracts more reliable. Contractors who gave variable rate contracts in previous years often passed steel price hikes to clients. A fixed-price contract with a reputable firm protects you from this.

Labour Availability

Hosur's industrial growth has also brought in a large skilled labour pool. Masons, carpenters, electricians, and plumbers are available locally. This keeps construction timelines more predictable compared to smaller towns where you might wait for specialist trades.
