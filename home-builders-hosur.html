What Goes Wrong

Why Building a Home
Goes Wrong in Hosur

Hosur has grown very quickly. That growth has brought many new builders and contractors into the market. Some are good. Many are not. These are the problems we hear about most often from families who come to us after a bad experience with another builder.

Low quote wins the project, high bill ends it

The most common issue with home builders in Hosur is the low initial quote that climbs steadily through the project. Window frames not included. Electrical conduits extra. Site levelling extra. Staircase railing extra. By the time the home is done, the final cost is 30 to 40 percent above what was quoted. A transparent line-item quote prevents this entirely.

Work stops when the advance runs out

Some builders in Hosur take a large advance, begin work, and then slow down or stop when the money is used up or diverted to another project. The family then has a partially built home, a contractor who is not returning calls, and no legal recourse because the agreement was verbal. Milestone-based payments tied to completed work prevent this.

Poor material quality hidden inside the structure

Concrete mix that does not match the specified grade. Reinforcement bars thinner than the drawing requires. Foundation depth less than what was agreed. These defects are not visible after the structure is complete. They show up years later in cracks, settlement, and dampness. A qualified architect on site during every structural stage catches these problems before they are covered up.

Design made up as construction proceeds

Many home builders in Hosur work without complete drawings. The floor plan exists for the approval. What actually gets built is decided during construction based on the contractor's experience. This leads to rooms that are too small, staircases that are too steep, kitchens that do not work well, and bathrooms that cannot be properly waterproofed because the drainage was not planned properly from the start.
